# Target LinkedIn Ad Audiences

## Prerequisites
- LinkedIn Campaign Manager account
- Defined ICP with firmographic and demographic criteria

## Steps

1. **Use LinkedIn's unique targeting.** LinkedIn's advantage is professional data: job title, seniority, company size, industry, skills, and group membership. No other ad platform has this level of B2B targeting. Use it to reach your exact ICP.

2. **Build your core audience.** Layer these targeting dimensions: Job Function (e.g., Engineering, IT) + Seniority (Director, VP, C-Suite) + Company Size (51-200, 201-500) + Industry (Computer Software, Internet). Each layer narrows the audience. Target 20K-80K audience size.

3. **Use job title targeting carefully.** Job titles are powerful but inconsistent ("VP of Engineering" vs "VP Engineering" vs "Vice President, Engineering"). Use Job Function + Seniority instead of exact titles for broader reach, or combine both for precision with adequate reach.

4. **Exclude irrelevant audiences.** Always exclude: your own company's employees, job seekers (if using seniority targeting), and audiences from previous campaigns that already converted. Exclusions prevent wasted spend on people who should not see the ad.

5. **Create Matched Audiences.** Upload your target account list (ABM list) as a Matched Audience. LinkedIn matches company names to their database. Combine with seniority targeting to reach decision-makers at your specific target accounts. Match rates are typically 60-80% for known companies.

6. **Test audience segments separately.** Create separate campaigns for different audience segments rather than combining them into one campaign. Example: one campaign targeting VPs at 50-200 employee companies, another targeting Directors at 200-500 employee companies. This reveals which segment performs best and lets you allocate budget accordingly.
