# Optimize LinkedIn Ad Bidding

## Prerequisites
- Active LinkedIn ad campaign with at least $500 spent
- Understanding of your target cost per lead or cost per click

## Steps

1. **Understand bidding options.** LinkedIn offers three bid strategies: Maximum Delivery (LinkedIn optimizes for most results within budget -- best for starting), Cost Cap (you set a target CPA and LinkedIn stays near it), and Manual Bidding (you set exact CPC or CPM -- for experienced advertisers).

2. **Start with Maximum Delivery.** For new campaigns, use Maximum Delivery and let LinkedIn's algorithm find the right bid. Run for 7 days and establish your baseline CPC and CPL. LinkedIn's B2B CPCs typically range from $5-15, and CPLs from $50-200 depending on audience and offer.

3. **Switch to Cost Cap for efficiency.** Once you know your baseline CPL, switch to Cost Cap with a target 10-20% below your baseline. Example: if your baseline CPL is $120, set Cost Cap to $100. LinkedIn will optimize delivery to hit this target. If delivery drops significantly, raise the cap slightly.

4. **Use Manual Bidding for precise control.** For mature campaigns where you know exact CPC values, use Manual CPC bidding. Set your bid at the suggested range's midpoint. Increase by $0.50 increments if delivery is too low. Decrease if you are spending budget too quickly with poor quality.

5. **Optimize by daypart.** Review performance by hour and day of week. If Tuesday morning consistently outperforms Friday afternoon, increase bids during high-performing windows and decrease during low performers. LinkedIn does not have native dayparting, so adjust budgets manually.

6. **Monitor auction competitiveness.** Check your campaign's "Audience Penetration" metric. If you are reaching less than 5% of your audience, your bids are too low. If you are reaching 30%+, you may be overbidding. Target 10-20% penetration for a healthy balance of reach and efficiency.
