# Set Up LinkedIn Ad Campaigns

## Prerequisites
- LinkedIn Campaign Manager account with billing configured
- LinkedIn Company Page linked to Campaign Manager
- Clear campaign objective and budget

## Steps

1. **Choose your campaign objective.** LinkedIn offers objectives aligned to funnel stages: Brand Awareness (top), Website Visits or Engagement (middle), Lead Generation or Conversions (bottom). For B2B GTM, start with Lead Generation (LinkedIn native forms) or Website Conversions (drive to your landing page).

2. **Create campaign groups.** Organize campaigns in groups by motion or audience. Example groups: "ABM - Target Accounts", "Content Promotion - ICP", "Retargeting - Website Visitors". Each group contains multiple campaigns with different audiences or creatives.

3. **Set up your first campaign.** In Campaign Manager, create a new campaign. Select your objective, then choose ad format: Single Image (simplest, good starting point), Carousel (for multi-point storytelling), or Message Ads (for direct outreach). Start with Single Image to establish baseline performance.

4. **Configure your audience.** Use LinkedIn's targeting (see `fundamentals/social/linkedin-ads-targeting`). Set audience size between 20K-100K for a good balance of reach and specificity. Enable Audience Expansion only if your targeting is very narrow (under 10K).

5. **Set budget and schedule.** Start with $50-100/day for testing. Run campaigns for at least 7 days before evaluating performance -- LinkedIn's algorithm needs time to optimize. Set a campaign end date to prevent runaway spend.

6. **Launch and optimize.** After launch, check performance daily for the first week. Key metrics: CTR (target >0.5% for sponsored content), CPL (cost per lead -- varies by industry but target under $100 for B2B SaaS), and conversion rate from click to lead. Pause underperforming ads after 1,000 impressions with below-threshold CTR.
