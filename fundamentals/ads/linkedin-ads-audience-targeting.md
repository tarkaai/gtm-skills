# Configure LinkedIn Ads Audience Targeting

### Step-by-step
1. In your LinkedIn campaign, go to the Audience section.
2. Start with company targeting: filter by industry (e.g., Computer Software), company size (e.g., 11-50, 51-200), and optionally specific company names.
3. Add job function targeting: select departments (Engineering, Marketing, Product) and seniority levels (VP, Director, Manager).
4. Alternatively, use job title targeting for precision: enter specific titles like 'VP of Engineering' or 'Head of Growth'. LinkedIn will suggest variations.
5. Add skills or interest targeting for additional refinement (e.g., people with 'Product Management' or 'Growth Marketing' skills).
6. Exclude audiences you don't want: competitors' employees, your own company, current customers.
7. Check the audience size: LinkedIn recommends 50,000-500,000 for Sponsored Content. If too small, broaden criteria; if too large, add more filters.
8. For ABM (Account-Based Marketing): upload a matched audience — a CSV of target company names or email addresses. LinkedIn matches these to profiles.
9. Create Lookalike audiences: upload a source audience (your best customers) and let LinkedIn find similar profiles.
10. Save your audience for reuse across campaigns, and create variations (e.g., 'ICP - Engineering Leaders' vs. 'ICP - Marketing Leaders') for testing.
