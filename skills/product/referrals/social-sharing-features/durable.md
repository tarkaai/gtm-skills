# Built-In Social Sharing — Durable Intelligence

> **Stage:** Product → Referrals | **Motion:** Lead Capture Surface | **Channels:** Product, Social

## Outcomes

The viral sharing loop runs on autopilot. The AI optimization agent continuously monitors the share funnel, K-factor, channel performance, and referral conversion. When any metric plateaus or declines, the agent diagnoses the cause, generates improvement hypotheses, runs A/B experiments, evaluates results, and auto-implements winners. The system sustains a K-factor of at least 0.10 and generates at least 100 viral signups per month over 6 months. The system converges toward the local maximum of viral growth for the current product and user base.

## Leading Indicators

- AI detects anomalies within 24 hours of metric deviation (daily health check classifies correctly)
- Hypotheses generated by the AI produce positive experiment results at least 40% of the time
- Weekly optimization briefs show cumulative positive AI lift trend
- Social share health monitor triggers fewer escalations over time (system stabilizing)
- K-factor sustains or improves quarter over quarter
- Convergence signal: successive experiments produce <2% improvement for 3 consecutive experiments

## Instructions

### 1. Deploy the autonomous optimization loop

Run the `autonomous-optimization` drill configured for the viral sharing system:

**Phase 1 — Monitor (daily via n8n cron):**
Configure the monitoring loop to track sharing-specific KPIs:
- Primary KPIs: share initiation rate (4-week rolling), share completion rate, share link CTR by channel, viral signup rate, K-factor (4-week rolling), viral signups per month, sharer retention rate
- Use `posthog-anomaly-detection` to compare last 2 weeks against 4-week rolling average
- Classification: normal (within +/-10%), plateau (+/-2% for 3+ weeks), drop (>20% decline), spike (>50% increase)
- If normal: log to Attio, no action
- If anomaly detected: trigger Phase 2

**Phase 2 — Diagnose (triggered by anomaly):**
Gather sharing system context from PostHog and Attio:
- Share funnel conversion rates by stage, by channel, by resource type
- Content variant performance (CTR by variant)
- OG card rendering validation (are previews still rendering correctly on each platform?)
- Referral reward effectiveness (reward recipients vs non-recipients share frequency)
- Recent experiment history and outcomes
Run `hypothesis-generation` with the anomaly data. Receive 3 ranked hypotheses. Examples:
- "Share link CTR on LinkedIn dropped 40% because LinkedIn changed their unfurling parser — updating OG meta tag format to match the new specification will restore CTR to baseline"
- "Share initiation rate plateaued because the share prompt is being shown at a moment when users are task-focused — moving the prompt to the post-completion celebration screen will increase initiation by 8pp"
- "K-factor is declining because share content has become stale — the same 3 content templates have been running for 6 weeks. Generating fresh variants with seasonal context will restore CTR by 12%"
- "Viral signup rate dropped because the shared resource landing page signup CTA was moved below the fold in last week's product redesign — restoring the CTA position will recover signup rate to prior levels"
If top hypothesis risk = "high" (e.g., changing the landing page structure, modifying the referral reward), escalate to human review and stop.

**Phase 3 — Experiment (triggered by hypothesis acceptance):**
Design and launch the experiment:
- Use `posthog-experiments` to create a feature flag splitting traffic between control and variant
- Implement the variant (e.g., new OG card template, adjusted prompt timing, fresh content variant, landing page CTA position)
- Minimum duration: 7 days or 100+ samples per variant (shares generate enough volume for standard sample sizes)
- Log experiment in Attio: hypothesis, start date, expected duration, success criteria
- Maximum 1 active experiment at a time per `autonomous-optimization` guardrails

**Phase 4 — Evaluate (triggered by experiment completion):**
Pull results from PostHog. Run `experiment-evaluation`:
- **Adopt**: improvement is statistically significant (95% confidence) and practically meaningful (>5% relative improvement). Roll out to 100%. Log the change.
- **Iterate**: result is directionally positive but not significant. Generate a refined hypothesis. Return to Phase 2.
- **Revert**: variant performed worse or no difference. Restore control. Log the failure. Return to Phase 1.
- **Extend**: insufficient sample size. Continue for another 7 days.
Store evaluation in Attio with decision, confidence level, and reasoning.

**Phase 5 — Report (weekly via n8n cron):**
Generate weekly optimization brief:
- Anomalies detected this week and classifications
- Hypotheses generated and their status
- Active experiment progress (sample size, preliminary direction)
- Completed experiments: adopted, reverted, or iterated
- Net metric change from all adopted changes this week
- K-factor trend with 12-week chart
- Viral signups trend with month-over-month comparison
- Current distance from estimated local maximum (based on improvement trend)
- Recommended focus for next week
Post to Slack and store in Attio.

### 2. Deploy the social share health monitor

Run the `social-share-health-monitor` drill for play-specific monitoring that complements the generic optimization loop:

- Configure daily health checks for all 8 sharing metrics: share surface impression rate, share initiation rate, share completion rate, share link CTR, viral signup rate, viral activation rate, K-factor, sharer retention
- Implement diagnostic triggers for each declining metric:
  - Share initiation declining: check button visibility (UI change?), compare by resource type, check user cohort mix
  - Share completion declining: check popover load time, compare by channel, verify clipboard API and third-party share URLs
  - Share link CTR declining: validate OG cards on each platform, compare CTR by channel, audit content generator output quality
  - Viral signup declining: check landing page load time and CTA visibility, verify attribution cookie, compare by channel traffic quality
  - K-factor declining: decompose into shares-per-user and conversion-per-share, diagnose the lagging component
- Configure automated interventions:
  - OG card broken: auto-trigger re-validation on all platforms, re-generate and purge CDN cache if rendering fails
  - Share prompt fatigue: reduce prompt frequency by 50% for cohorts with declining share completion rates
  - Stale content: switch to a new content generation prompt variant if CTR drops below template baseline
  - Viral drop revival: trigger re-engagement campaign to top 50 sharers with personalized "your shares got X views" nudge
- Build weekly health report: aggregate daily checks, share funnel trend analysis, channel performance breakdown, K-factor decomposition, top shared resources
- Set escalation rules: any metric critical 3+ days, K-factor below 0.05 for 2 weeks, share completion rate below 20% for 1 week, 3+ interventions in one week with no improvement, OG rendering broken on 2+ platforms

### 3. Monitor for convergence

The optimization loop runs indefinitely. Watch for convergence signals:

- 3 consecutive experiments produce <2% improvement on the target metric
- All 8 health metrics stable in "healthy" range for 4+ consecutive weeks
- Weekly optimization briefs consistently report "no anomalies detected"
- K-factor variance is <5% week-over-week for 6+ consecutive weeks

At convergence:
1. The viral sharing system has reached its local maximum
2. Reduce monitoring frequency from daily to weekly
3. Reduce experiment cadence from continuous to monthly spot-checks
4. Report: "Social Sharing is optimized at [current metrics]. K-factor: [value]. Viral signups: [N]/month. Share initiation rate: [N]%. Top channel: [channel] at [CTR]% CTR. Further gains require strategic changes (new shareable resource types, product-level virality features, platform integrations) rather than tactical optimization."

### 4. Handle structural changes

If the optimization loop stalls (4 consecutive failed experiments) or health metrics enter critical despite optimization:

**Human action required:** The agent flags this for strategic review. Possible structural changes:
- Add new shareable resource types: collaborative workspaces, public galleries, user portfolios, team leaderboards
- Build platform-specific integrations: native Twitter/X embed, LinkedIn article integration, Slack app with share previews, Discord bot
- Add collaborative virality: "Work on this together" invitations that bring new users directly into a shared workspace
- Implement competitive/social features: public user profiles, community showcases, "trending shared content" feed
- Explore new share channels: WhatsApp share, SMS share, QR codes on printed exports, shareable email signatures with product branding
- Redesign the referral reward structure: switch from credits to tangible benefits, add gamification (sharing leaderboard with monthly prizes)

After structural changes are implemented, reset the optimization baseline and restart the loop.
