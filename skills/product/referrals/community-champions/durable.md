# Champion Recognition Program — Durable Intelligence

> **Stage:** Product → Referrals | **Motion:** Lead Capture Surface | **Channels:** Product, Email, Social

## Outcomes

The champion program runs on autopilot. The AI optimization agent continuously monitors enrollment, referral activation, co-marketing output, community impact, and champion retention. When any metric plateaus or declines, the agent diagnoses the cause, generates improvement hypotheses, runs A/B experiments, evaluates results, and auto-implements winners. The program sustains at least 30 active champion referrers producing 2+ referrals per quarter each over 6 months. The system converges toward the local maximum of champion-driven referral growth for the current community.

## Leading Indicators

- AI detects anomalies within 24 hours of metric deviation (daily health check classifies correctly)
- Hypotheses generated by the AI produce positive experiment results at least 40% of the time
- Weekly optimization briefs show cumulative positive AI lift trend
- Champion program health monitor triggers fewer escalations over time (system stabilizing)
- Referral conversion rate sustains or improves quarter over quarter
- Community champion pool grows in proportion to overall community growth
- Convergence signal: successive experiments produce <2% improvement for 3 consecutive experiments

## Instructions

### 1. Deploy the autonomous optimization loop

Run the `autonomous-optimization` drill configured for the champion recognition program:

**Phase 1 — Monitor (daily via n8n cron):**
Configure the monitoring loop to track champion-specific KPIs:
- Primary KPIs: active champion referrer count (90-day rolling), referral link activation rate (30-day), referral conversion rate (4-week rolling), referrals per champion per quarter, revenue attributed to champion referrals, co-marketing content published per month
- Use `posthog-anomaly-detection` to compare last 2 weeks against 4-week rolling average
- Classification: normal (within +/-10%), plateau (+/-2% for 3+ weeks), drop (>20% decline), spike (>50% increase)
- If normal: log to Attio, no action
- If anomaly detected: trigger Phase 2

**Phase 2 — Diagnose (triggered by anomaly):**
Gather champion program context from PostHog and Attio:
- Current tier distribution and per-tier referral rates
- Enrollment funnel conversion rates by week
- Referral nudge sequence performance (open rates, click rates, share rates)
- Co-marketing invitation acceptance rates and content performance
- Community health metrics: overall activity trend, new member growth, question volume
- Recent experiment history and outcomes
Run `hypothesis-generation` with the anomaly data. Receive 3 ranked hypotheses. Examples:
- "Referral activation dropped because the social amplification kit content is stale after 2 months — refreshing with new product announcements and a seasonal campaign will restore activation by 10pp"
- "Enrollment rate declined because the community scoring threshold of 50 is too high for the current community size — lowering to 40 will expand the pipeline by 40% without degrading referral quality"
- "Co-marketing acceptance dropped because champions are being asked for blog posts (high effort) when their dimension scores indicate they prefer social amplification (low effort) — re-matching opportunities will improve acceptance by 15pp"
- "Champion retention declined because the early feature access perk has not been refreshed in 3 months — rotating the featured early access feature will re-engage lapsed champions"
If top hypothesis risk = "high" (e.g., lowering threshold, changing tier structure, modifying incentive economics), escalate to human review and stop.

**Phase 3 — Experiment (triggered by hypothesis acceptance):**
Design and launch the experiment:
- Use `posthog-experiments` to create a feature flag splitting traffic between control and variant
- Implement the variant (e.g., refreshed social kit, adjusted threshold, new perk, re-matched opportunities)
- Minimum duration: 7 days or 30+ champions per variant (champion program experiments have smaller sample sizes than product-wide experiments)
- Log experiment in Attio: hypothesis, start date, expected duration, success criteria
- Maximum 1 active experiment at a time per `autonomous-optimization` guardrails

**Phase 4 — Evaluate (triggered by experiment completion):**
Pull results from PostHog. Run `experiment-evaluation`:
- **Adopt**: improvement is statistically significant (90% confidence for small-sample champion tests) and practically meaningful (>5% relative improvement). Roll out to 100%. Log the change.
- **Iterate**: result is directionally positive but not significant. Generate a refined hypothesis. Return to Phase 2.
- **Revert**: variant performed worse or no difference. Restore control. Log the failure. Return to Phase 1.
- **Extend**: insufficient sample size. Continue for another 7 days.
Store evaluation in Attio with decision, confidence level, and reasoning.

**Phase 5 — Report (weekly via n8n cron):**
Generate weekly optimization brief:
- Anomalies detected this week and classifications
- Hypotheses generated and their status
- Active experiment progress (sample size, preliminary direction)
- Completed experiments: adopted, reverted, or iterated
- Net metric change from all adopted changes this week
- Current distance from estimated local maximum (based on improvement trend)
- Community health context: is the overall community growing, stable, or shrinking? Champion program metrics must be interpreted relative to community health.
- Recommended focus for next week
Post to Slack and store in Attio.

### 2. Deploy the champion program health monitor

Run the `champion-program-health-monitor` drill for play-specific monitoring that complements the generic optimization loop:

- Configure daily health checks for all 8 champion program metrics: champion pool growth, enrollment rate, referral activation rate, referral conversion, co-marketing acceptance, champion retention, champion score stability, content yield
- Implement diagnostic triggers for each declining metric:
  - Pool growth declining: check if overall community activity is shrinking (upstream) vs scoring thresholds too high (calibration drift)
  - Enrollment rate declining: check enrollment message delivery and click rates, compare by community channel
  - Referral activation declining: check if champions are seeing the referral ask vs seeing but not sharing vs sharing but links not converting
  - Co-marketing acceptance declining: check if opportunity matching aligns with champion strengths, review ask frequency against burnout guardrails
  - Champion retention declining: cross-reference with product usage to distinguish program churn from product churn from community churn
- Configure automated interventions:
  - Stale enrollment message: auto-rotate to next variant if click rate drops below 5%
  - Referral activation stall: personalized impact-stat email showing the champion's community contribution with referral CTA as secondary
  - Co-marketing drought: auto-switch from high-effort asks (blog) to low-effort asks (social amplification kit) for all champions
  - Lapsed champion: re-engagement message referencing the community topics they used to help with
- Build weekly health report: aggregate daily checks, trend analysis, intervention outcomes, notable achievements
- Set escalation rules: any metric critical 3+ days, champion retention below 55% for 2 weeks, referral conversion below 12% for 4 weeks, 3+ interventions in one week with no improvement

### 3. Monitor for convergence

The optimization loop runs indefinitely. Watch for convergence signals:

- 3 consecutive experiments produce <2% improvement on the target metric
- All 8 health metrics stable in "healthy" range for 4+ consecutive weeks
- Weekly optimization briefs consistently report "no anomalies detected"

At convergence:
1. The champion program has reached its local maximum
2. Reduce monitoring frequency from daily to weekly
3. Reduce experiment cadence from continuous to monthly spot-checks
4. Report: "Champion Recognition Program is optimized at [current metrics]. Active referrers: [N]. Referrals per quarter: [N]. Referral conversion: [N]%. Revenue attributed: $[N]/mo. Co-marketing content: [N] pieces/quarter. Further gains require strategic changes (new community platforms, new co-marketing formats, product-level virality features) rather than tactical optimization."

### 4. Handle structural changes

If the optimization loop stalls (4 consecutive failed experiments) or health metrics enter critical despite optimization:

**Human action required:** The agent flags this for strategic review. Possible structural changes:
- Expand to new community platforms: if the current community channels are saturated, launch the program in new platforms (Reddit, X, LinkedIn groups, Hacker News)
- Add new co-marketing formats: podcast appearances, video testimonials, conference talks, partner community cross-pollination
- Redesign the incentive structure: switch from product credits to tangible rewards, add gamification (champion leaderboard with quarterly prizes), or introduce a formal ambassador program with paid stipends
- Integrate with product virality: built-in sharing features, public galleries of champion-created content, "recommended by [Champion]" badges on shared resources
- Evolve the scoring model: add new dimensions (social reach outside the community, NPS correlation, revenue influence), different weights, cross-platform contribution aggregation
- Community platform strategy: if the community itself is shrinking, the champion program cannot compensate. The upstream problem (community health) must be addressed first.

After structural changes are implemented, reset the optimization baseline and restart the loop.
