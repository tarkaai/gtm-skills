# Comment-to-DM Play — Smoke Test

> **Stage:** Marketing → Problem Aware | **Motion:** Founder Social Content | **Channels:** Social

## Outcomes

Prove that commenting on ICP prospects' LinkedIn posts builds enough familiarity to open DM conversations that lead to meetings. At Smoke, the founder runs the entire process manually with agent assistance for preparation. Success = at least 3 DM conversations started and 1 meeting booked in a single week.

## Leading Indicators

- Comments posted per day (target: 5-8 per day)
- Author reply rate on your comments (target: ≥ 20% of comments get an author reply)
- Profile views trending up during the comment week
- Connection requests received from people you commented on

## Instructions

### 1. Define your comment-to-DM ICP

Run the `icp-definition` drill to establish who you are targeting. For this play, you need an additional filter: prospects who are ACTIVE on LinkedIn (post at least weekly). An ideal prospect who never posts on LinkedIn is useless for this motion. Document: target titles, company sizes, industries, and the 3-5 pain points you can speak to in comments.

### 2. Build your initial comment target list

Run the `prospect-content-discovery` drill (Option A -- Manual) to build a list of 15-20 LinkedIn profiles:
- 10 direct ICP prospects (people who could buy your product and post on LinkedIn)
- 5 industry peers whose audience contains your ICP
- 5 big accounts (10K+ followers) in your space

Turn on LinkedIn bell notifications for each Tier 1 prospect. Follow all Tier 2 and Tier 3 accounts. This takes 30-45 minutes.

### 3. Execute 5 days of commenting

Run the `comment-crafting` drill daily for 5 business days:

**Each morning (20-30 minutes):**
1. Check LinkedIn notifications for new posts from your target list
2. Browse your LinkedIn feed for 10 minutes to find additional posts from ICP-adjacent accounts
3. Select 5-8 posts to comment on
4. Write and post a thoughtful comment on each using the comment-crafting strategies (add value 60%, counterpoint 20%, sharp question 15%, personal story 5%)

**Each evening (5 minutes):**
5. Check which comments got author replies or likes
6. Reply to any authors who responded to your comments (keep the conversation going)
7. Log results: post URL, author, your comment, whether they replied

**Human action required:** The founder must personally write or review and post each comment. AI can draft comments, but the founder must ensure they sound authentic and match their voice.

### 4. Identify DM-ready prospects

By day 3-5, identify prospects showing recognition signals:
- They replied to your comment at least once
- They liked 2+ of your comments
- They sent you a connection request
- They visited your profile

These prospects are warm enough for a DM. Expect 3-6 DM-ready prospects from 5 days of commenting on 15-20 accounts.

### 5. Send DMs to warm prospects

For each DM-ready prospect, send a first DM that references your shared comment history:

"Hey {name}, really enjoyed the exchange on your post about {topic}. Your point about {specific thing} stuck with me. Curious how you are approaching {related challenge}? Would love to hear more."

**Rules:** Do not pitch. Do not mention your product. Ask about their situation. Keep it to 3-4 sentences.

**Human action required:** The founder sends each DM personally. This is a relationship-building moment, not an automated outreach step.

### 6. Evaluate against threshold

Run the `threshold-engine` drill to measure: ≥ 3 DM conversations started AND ≥ 1 meeting booked in 1 week. Count a "DM conversation" as a DM where the prospect replied with a substantive message (not just "thanks").

If PASS, proceed to Baseline. If FAIL, diagnose: Was it comment quality (low reply rate)? Wrong targets (not ICP)? Premature DMs (not enough warming touches)? Adjust and re-run.

## Time Estimate

- ICP definition: 1 hour
- Target list building: 45 minutes
- Daily commenting (5 days x 30 min): 2.5 hours
- DM crafting and sending: 30 minutes
- Evaluation: 15 minutes
- **Total: ~5 hours over 1 week**
